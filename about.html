About Phi Alpha Theta

Phi Alpha Theta is an inclusive community dedicated to fostering a deep appreciation for history among its members. Our mission is to provide history-related opportunities and academic support to students in history courses. Through engaging activities, competitions, and tutoring, we create an environment where historical knowledge thrives.

Our purpose is to enrich the academic experience by offering diverse history-focused avenues and extending support to students facing history challenges. By nurturing a love for history and facilitating academic growth, we aim to make a positive impact on the educational journey of all involved at Carnegie Vanguard.

Bylaws

Article I: Name and Purpose

Section 1: Name

The name of this organization shall be Phi Alpha Theta.

Section 2: Purpose

The purpose of Phi Alpha Theta is to provide all of its members with access to diverse history-related opportunities and to offer academic assistance in the form of tutoring to students struggling in their history courses.

Article II: Membership

Section 1: Eligibility

Membership in Phi Alpha Theta is open to all students who share an interest in history, maintain above an 85 in their history classes, which will be evaluated at the end of every academic cycle, and are enrolled at Carnegie Vanguard High School.

Section 2: Rights and Responsibilities

Members have the right to participate in all club activities, competitions, and events. Members are responsible for upholding the values of academic integrity, respect, and inclusivity. If these values are put into question by any other ΦΑΘ member, they will need to meet with the ΦΑΘ Cabinet for possible dismissal, pending review of said grievance.

Article III: Activities

Section 1: Main Activities

Phi Alpha Theta's main activities shall include participation in History Day Competitions, hosting historical presentations, historical engagement in UIL Academic History Competitions, organizing a School-Wide Geography Bee, and providing tutoring support to fellow students struggling in history courses.

Section 2: Additional Activities

The club may organize guest speaker sessions, historical field trips, debates, trivia, and other events to enhance and demonstrate members' historical knowledge and experiences.

Article IV: Leadership

Section 1: Election

Officers shall be elected by club members annually, following procedures outlined in the election bylaws. If a cabinet member consistently fails to meet their duties, the other cabinet members and the sponsor can move to dismiss the cabinet member from the club.

Section 2: Officer Roles and Duties

The leadership of Phi Alpha Theta shall consist of a President, Vice President, Secretary, and Treasurer, each with specific duties and responsibilities.

Section 3: President

The President shall:

  • Provide overall leadership and direction to the club.
  • Preside over meetings and ensure their orderly execution.
  • Coordinate and oversee club activities, events, and projects.
  • Serve as the primary liaison between the club and school administration.
  • Foster a welcoming and inclusive environment for all members.
  • Represent Phi Alpha Theta in official capacities as needed.

Section 4: Vice President

The Vice President shall:

  • Support the President in their duties and responsibilities.
  • Assume the role of the President in their absence.
  • Assist in organizing and executing club activities, events, and projects.
  • Collaborate with committee chairs and members to ensure smooth operation.
  • Contribute to maintaining a positive and enthusiastic club atmosphere.

Section 5: Secretary

The Secretary shall:

  • Maintain accurate records of club meetings, activities, and minutes.
  • Communicate important club information, announcements, and updates to members and school officials through Remind and Instagram.
  • Assist in correspondence with external parties, including other organizations and speakers.
  • Keep track of member attendance.
  • Manage club documents and records, ensuring they are organized and accessible.

Section 6: Treasurer

The Treasurer shall:

  • Manage the club's financial matters, including budgeting and fundraising.
  • Maintain a record of club expenditures, income, and financial transactions.
  • Provide regular financial reports to the club membership and school officials.
  • Seek and explore funding opportunities to support club initiatives and historical field trips.
  • Ensure financial transparency and accountability within the organization.

Article V: Meetings

Section 1: Frequency

Regular club meetings shall be held biweekly and may be adjusted based on members' availability.

Section 2: Quorum and Attendance

A quorum shall comprise of at least 66% of active club members. Members must attend all club meetings. However, if unable to attend, members must contact a cabinet member. After three unexcused absences, a club member will be dismissed.

Article VI: Amendments

Section 1: Proposal

Any member may propose amendments to these bylaws.

Section 2: Approval

All proposed bylaw amendments will be subject to review by present club members. Amendments shall be approved by at least a 66% majority vote of present club members.

These bylaws are hereby adopted by Phi Alpha Theta to govern its activities, operations, and interactions. The organization shall operate by the principles and guidelines set forth herein.
